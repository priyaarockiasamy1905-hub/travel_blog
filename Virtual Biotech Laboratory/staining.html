STAINING

Procedure

Simple staining

  1. Spread bacteria with a drop of water on a slide.
  2. Let the slide air-dry completely.
  3. Pass the slide through a flame 2 to 3 times to stick the cells.
  4. Flood the smear with a dye (like Methylene Blue) for 1 minute.
  5. Gently wash off the excess dye with water.
  6. Tap dry with absorbent paper without rubbing.
  7. Observe the cells under a microscope.

Gram staining

  1. Spread bacteria with water on a slide and let it air-dry.
  2. Pass the slide through a flame 2 to 3 times to stick the cells.
  3. Flood with Crystal Violet for 1 minute, then rinse with water.
  4. Flood with Iodine for 1 minute to lock the color, then rinse.
  5. Rinse with Alcohol for 5-10 seconds, then immediately wash with water.
  6. Flood with Safranin for 1 minute, then rinse with water.
  7. Tap dry with absorbent paper and observe under a microscope.
