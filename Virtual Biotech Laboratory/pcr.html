PCR Technique

Procedure

  1. Combine the target DNA, primers, nucleotides (dNTPs), DNA polymerase, and buffer in a small PCR tube.
  2. Heat the mixture to 94-98°C for 20-30 seconds to separate the double-stranded DNA into single strands.
  3. Cool the mixture to 50-65°C for 20-40 seconds so the primers can bind to the single-stranded DNA templates.
  4. Heat the mixture to 72°C to allow the DNA polymerase to synthesize and build the new DNA strands.
  5. Run this heating and cooling cycle 25 to 35 times to exponentially copy the DNA.
  6. Cool the final mixture down to 4°C to safely store the copied DNA products.
