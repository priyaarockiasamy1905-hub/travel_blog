Cell Culture

Procedure

  1. Sterilise the workspace by spraying a laminar flow hood with 70% alcohol and turning on UV light to kill any contaminants.
  2. Thaw the cells rapidly in a warm water bath and transfer them into a tube with fresh, nutrient-rich liquid growth medium.
  3. Spin the cells in a centrifuge to separate them from the toxic freezing chemicals, then throw away the old liquid.
  4. Plate the cells by mixing the clean cell pellet with fresh growth medium and pouring them into a sterile culture flask.
  5. Incubate the cells inside a warm, controlled oven at 37°C with 5% carbon dioxide so they can attach and multiply.
  6. Feed and split the cells every few days by removing old medium, rinsing them, and dividing them into new flasks before they overcrowd.
