Isolation of DNA

Procedure

  1. Break open the cells by mashing your sample and mixing it with a soap solution to pop the cell membranes.
  2. Separate the liquid from the heavy cell waste by spinning the mixture in a centrifuge.
  3. Clump the DNA together by adding ice-cold alcohol to the liquid until white, stringy strands appear.
  4. Wash the DNA by spinning it again into a small pellet and rinsing it with 70% alcohol to remove impurities.
  5. Dissolve the DNA by letting the alcohol air-dry and mixing the clean pellet into water or storage buffer.
