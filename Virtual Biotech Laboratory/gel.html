Gel Electrophoresis

Procedure

  1. Make the gel by melting agarose powder in a buffer solution, pouring it into a tray, and inserting a comb to create small wells.
  2. Set up the chamber by removing the comb once the gel hardens, placing the gel inside the tank, and filling it with an electrical buffer liquid.
  3. Load the samples by mixing your DNA with a heavy colored dye and carefully pipetting it into the wells alongside a size standard ruler.
  4. Run the current by plugging in the power supply so the negative electrical charge pushes the DNA toward the positive red electrode.
  5. Stop the run when the colored tracking dye moves near the end of the gel, turning off the electricity before the DNA falls off the edge.
  6. View the DNA by staining the gel with a fluorescent dye and placing it under a UV light box to see the glowing bands.
