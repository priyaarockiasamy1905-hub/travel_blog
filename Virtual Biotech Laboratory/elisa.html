ELISA

Procedure

  1. Coat the microplate well with your target sample.
  2. Block remaining empty spaces with an unrelated protein.
  3. Wash the plate with buffer to clear unbound molecules.
  4. Incubate with a specific primary antibody.
  5. Wash the plate again to remove excess antibody.
  6. Add an enzyme-linked secondary antibody.
  7. Wash the plate to remove unbound secondary antibodies.
  8. Introduce a chemical substrate to trigger a color change.
  9. Stop the enzymatic reaction using an acid or base.
  10. Measure the color intensity with a microplate reader.
